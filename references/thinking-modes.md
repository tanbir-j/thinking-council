# Thinking Modes

This skill adapts Tey Bannerman's Four Modes framework for article review. The source framework describes Compression as finding clarity when overwhelmed, Expansion as generating options when stuck, Reflection as challenging reasoning, and Execution as producing once the thinking is ready.

Source: https://fourmodes.teybannerman.com/

## Compression

Purpose: find the signal, governing thesis, and load-bearing structure.

Look for:

- too many competing ideas
- repeated claims
- frameworks with unnecessary parts
- unclear hierarchy
- sections that do not serve the thesis

Typical counsel: Aristotle, Occam.

Do not compress merely to make the article shorter. Preserve nuance, evidence, rhythm, humour, and distinctive examples that earn their place.

## Expansion

Purpose: move beyond default assumptions and reveal stronger possibilities.

Look for:

- a competent but predictable argument
- missing implications
- unexplored tensions
- weak examples or analogies
- narrow audience or strategic framing
- an idea that has not yet become a contribution

Typical counsel: Plato, Hegel, Nietzsche, Kuhn.

Expansion should enlarge the idea, not add random sections.

## Reflection

Purpose: stress-test reasoning before the market does it more publicly.

Look for:

- undefined terms
- unsupported claims
- hidden assumptions
- plausible counterexamples
- selective evidence
- contradictions or boundary conditions

Typical counsel: Socrates, Popper, supported by Sherlock.

## Execution

Purpose: convert diagnosis into a manageable revision sequence.

Look for:

- correct diagnosis with no next action
- recommendations that are too broad
- changes attempted in the wrong order
- editing prose before resolving the proposition

Typical leads: Harvey, Ted, and Mary, with Thrawn preserving the whole and Sherlock protecting evidence.

## Mode diagnosis

State:

- which modes dominate the current artefact
- which modes are missing
- which mode should lead the next revision cycle
- the selected bench and why

Directional percentages may be useful, for example:

> Required next: Expansion 40%, Reflection 30%, Compression 20%, Execution 10%.

Do not present percentages as measured science. They are a way to make the review's emphasis visible.

## Common patterns

### Coherent but ordinary

Lead with Expansion, then Reflection. Avoid premature Compression.

### Original but sprawling

Lead with Compression and Thrawn's architecture review. Protect distinctive material.

### Persuasive but weakly evidenced

Lead with Reflection through Sherlock, Socrates, and Popper.

### Intellectually sound but difficult to read

Lead with Execution through Mary and Ted, then use Occam selectively.

### Near-final and apparently strong

Lead with Reflection and evidence-backed originality. Use Compression only for final cuts.
