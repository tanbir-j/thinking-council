# Author Positioning Charter

Use this reference for Tanbir's personal-brand assessment. Treat it as a starting charter and update the judgement when the user provides newer published work or a revised positioning statement.

## Core territory

Finance, analytics, strategy, and operations, with finance and analytics transformation as the centre of gravity.

## Primary audience

CFOs, finance leaders, and business decision-makers.

## Central promise

Help leaders drive the finance function forward and become credible changemakers. Show how finance and analytics transformation can be delivered with pace and precision without discarding control, evidence, or implementation reality.

## Authority model

The work should combine:

- a battle-hardened practitioner who understands what survives contact with systems, politics, data, people, and month-end
- ex-consultancy thought leadership that structures the problem, builds a defensible argument, and creates reusable frameworks

Flag imbalance:

- practitioner content becoming anecdotal or parochial
- consulting structure becoming polished but untouched by delivery reality

## Voice and presentation

- plain, direct, and measured
- intellectually grounded without sounding academic
- light-hearted where the subject allows
- dry or dark humour used with control
- analogies and pop-culture references used to explain, not decorate
- confident without declaring every idea revolutionary
- practical detail and concrete consequences

## Recurring themes

- driving the finance function forward
- finance leaders becoming changemakers
- turning strategy into operational delivery
- using analytics and AI to improve decisions and execution
- combining pace with control and precision
- closing the gap between theoretical transformation and what organisations can deliver

## Brand-strengthening signals

An article should normally do at least two of the following:

1. Introduce a distinctive way to think about finance or analytics transformation.
2. Convert a broad idea into a practical method.
3. Connect finance with strategy, technology, behaviour, operations, or organisational change.
4. Challenge a comfortable assumption held by finance leaders.
5. Provide language, frameworks, or analogies readers may reuse.
6. Demonstrate lessons earned through implementation rather than observation.

## Brand risks

Flag content that:

- drifts into generic AI or management commentary without a finance or decision anchor
- repeats familiar transformation advice without a new mechanism or perspective
- claims more than the evidence or experience earns
- uses consulting structure without practitioner substance
- uses pop culture as a gimmick
- chases a topical subject that does not strengthen the wider body of work
- promises pace while ignoring controls, data quality, ownership, capability, or implementation constraints

## Personal-brand test

Assess four questions:

### Territory

Does the article reinforce or credibly extend finance, analytics, strategy, or operations?

### Authority

Does it demonstrate both structured thinking and practical experience?

### Recognition

Does it contain an idea, phrase, framework, or analogy that readers could associate with Tanbir?

### Cumulative value

Does it advance the body of work, connect existing themes, or merely add another publication?

## Body-of-work research

When available, search the user's uploaded work rather than assuming its contents. Compare the article for:

- repetition without advancement
- development of an earlier idea
- coherent links between themes
- contradiction that should be explained
- a potential signature framework

If the body of work is incomplete, state that the personal-brand verdict is provisional.
