# Output Formats

Adapt headings when the material demands it, but preserve the decisions and verdicts below.

## Council Glance

Use this compact structure:

### Mode selected

State the artefact stage, intended outcome, audience, and why Council Glance applies.

### Immediate verdict

Give the direct judgement in one short paragraph.

### What is working

Identify the strongest idea or asset to protect.

### Where it falls short

Give three to five findings. For each:

- finding
- why it matters
- practical improvement

### Thinking balance

State the next required balance of Compression, Expansion, Reflection, and Execution. Name only the selected philosophers.

### Positioning verdicts

Give editorial verdicts for innovation, thought leadership, and personal brand. State what would raise each by one level.

### Next move

Name the single highest-value next action.

## Full Council

### 1. Mode and assignment

State operating mode, article stage, audience, objective, desired reader response, and any inferred assumptions.

### 2. Overall verdict

Explain how close the article is to achieving its objective. Identify the main constraint.

### 3. What must be protected

Name the distinctive idea, passage, analogy, voice, evidence, or structure that revisions must preserve.

### 4. Harness Team assessment

Give one integrated assessment across:

- strategic context and problem definition
- evidence and inference
- positioning and persuasion
- audience, people, and change
- practical clarity and usability

### 5. Article scorecard

Score the eight mature-profile dimensions from 1 to 5 with a brief rationale. Do not average.

### 6. Structural diagnosis

Cover:

- thesis
- narrative thread in one sentence
- coherence
- selective MECE
- section sequence
- opening, transitions, and conclusion
- plain-language barriers

### 7. Thinking-mode diagnosis and appointed counsel

State the current and required mode balance. Include only counsel that adds a distinct finding. Explain disagreements where they reveal a real choice.

### 8. Priority improvement agenda

Rank three to five actions using Reconsider, Develop, Restructure, Clarify, Cut, and Protect. State impact and suggested sequence.

### 9. Verdicts

Give separate verdicts for:

- publication readiness
- innovation
- thought leadership
- personal brand

For each positioning verdict, state what would raise it by one level.

## Publication Tribunal

Use the Full Council structure, then add:

### Evidence-backed originality review

For each material comparable source, state:

- the overlapping claim, framework, language, or analogy
- the degree of overlap
- what remains distinctive in the manuscript
- whether attribution, reframing, or a narrower claim is needed

Synthesize the research into one of:

- original idea
- original synthesis
- original application
- fresh communication
- material prior art

State confidence and limitations. Cite all material external claims.

### Body-of-work fit

When uploaded work is available, state whether this manuscript repeats, develops, connects, contradicts, extends, or creates signature territory.

### Final publication decision

Give one clear decision and the minimum changes required before publication.

## Comparison review

When comparing versions, use:

1. Shared objective
2. Winner by dimension
3. Trade-offs
4. Material worth preserving from each
5. Hybrid recommendation, if stronger
6. Final choice

Do not declare a winner without explaining the cost of the choice.

## Revision review

Use:

1. Issues resolved
2. Issues still open
3. New problems introduced
4. Distinctive material preserved or lost
5. Updated scores and verdicts only where changed
6. Publication decision
