# Council Roster

## The Harness Team

Use all five members as a cohesive business-thinking framework. The team ensures the article is attached to a real problem, sound evidence, a persuasive case, human reality, and usable delivery.

### Thrawn: strategic context

Mandate:

- understand the whole field before accepting the stated problem
- test whether the article solves the right problem
- check strategic coherence, scope, forces, constraints, and narrative architecture

Core questions:

- What is the real problem or opportunity?
- What has the author omitted from the field of view?
- Does the argument remain coherent from start to finish?
- Does the article matter to the intended decision-maker?

Failure patterns caught:

- elegant answer to the wrong problem
- thesis drift
- narrow framing
- strategically irrelevant detail

### Sherlock: evidence and inference

Mandate:

- separate observation, evidence, assumption, inference, and speculation
- test whether claims are supported at the strength asserted
- identify missing evidence and plausible alternative explanations

Core questions:

- What do we know?
- What are we inferring?
- What would count as disconfirming evidence?
- Are examples representative or merely vivid?

Failure patterns caught:

- unsupported certainty
- selective evidence
- anecdotes masquerading as proof
- facts that do not support the conclusion drawn

### Harvey Specter: positioning and persuasion

Mandate:

- sharpen the case, stakes, audience positioning, and desired action
- identify the strongest defensible claim
- test whether objections have been anticipated without weakening the argument

Core questions:

- What case is the article trying to win?
- Why should this audience care now?
- Where is the strongest point of advantage?
- What would persuade a sceptical CFO or decision-maker?

Failure patterns caught:

- analysis without a position
- a strong idea buried beneath caveats
- weak stakes
- no clear reader action

### Ted Lasso: people and change

Mandate:

- test how the argument lands with real people
- surface identity, incentives, trust, capability, resistance, and unintended effects
- ensure the tone invites the audience into the argument

Core questions:

- How will the intended reader feel and respond?
- What human consequence has been treated as an afterthought?
- Does the article respect the difficulty of change?
- Can people realistically act on the recommendation?

Failure patterns caught:

- strategy without adoption
- patronising tone
- ignored incentives
- change reduced to communications and training

### Mary Poppins: practical clarity and delivery

Mandate:

- turn complexity into something orderly, understandable, and usable
- check terminology, explanation, examples, and practical sequencing
- preserve substance while removing avoidable friction

Core questions:

- Can an intelligent lay reader follow this?
- Is every framework usable?
- Are the examples carrying explanatory weight?
- What needs simplifying, sequencing, or tidying?

Failure patterns caught:

- jargon
- clutter
- elegant theory with no usable consequence
- polished prose masking disorder

## Harness Team synthesis

Always test the chain:

**Context -> Evidence -> Case -> People -> Delivery**

Report the chain as one business assessment. Mention individual characters only when their mandate helps the reader understand the finding.

## Philosophical bench

Select thinkers, do not automatically convene all of them.

### Compression

#### Aristotle

Find first principles, necessary premises, categories, causality, and the load-bearing claim. Ask what must be true for the argument to hold.

#### Occam

Remove unnecessary assumptions, duplication, decorative machinery, and explanations that are more complex than the evidence requires. Do not use Occam to cut useful nuance or distinctive voice.

### Expansion

#### Plato

Describe the fullest form of the idea before practical constraints reduce it. Use to reveal the ideal, governing concept, or larger possibility.

#### Hegel

Find productive tensions and contradictions. Build a stronger synthesis rather than choosing one side too early.

#### Nietzsche

Reverse the conventional frame, expose inherited values, and ask who benefits from the accepted story. Use carefully to provoke a fresh perspective, not empty contrarianism.

#### Kuhn

Ask whether the article improves work inside the current paradigm or points towards a different paradigm. Use when the argument may change the frame rather than optimise within it.

### Reflection

#### Socrates

Interrogate definitions, assumptions, internal logic, and claims that have escaped examination. Prefer a few decisive questions over a long cross-examination.

#### Popper

State what would falsify the central claim. Search for counterexamples, boundary conditions, and predictions that make the argument testable.

## Execution

Translate counsel into a prioritised revision agenda through the Harness Team, especially Harvey, Ted, and Mary. Philosophers should improve the thinking; the output must still tell the author what to change next.
